About the Energy Map

The Clean Air Task Force seeks to meet the world’s rising energy demand in a way that is financially, socially, and environmentally sustainable. As an organization that advocates for the technology and policy changes needed to achieve a zero-emissions, high-energy planet, we strive for global futures where emerging economies invest in clean energy. Specifically in sub-saharan Africa, where 80% of the world’s population without electricity lives, there remains a crucial gap in addressing the challenges and opportunities regarding energy technology deployment and readiness. The composite index displayed by this map seeks to fill a gap in existing reports by providing a comprehensive assessment of the current state of energy technology deployment in Africa while also incorporating critical factors such as country planning, potential resources, grid infrastructure, policy, and innovation.

Sandbox Project Overview

The Africa Energy Map draws from a comprehensive range of reliable sources including national energy ministries, international organizations, academic research, and industry reports to provide the most accurate representation of the continent's energy landscape.

Methodology

Overview

Our assessment framework evaluates countries based on three primary dimensions, each containing multiple subdimensions that provide a comprehensive understanding of a nation's energy landscape. The data comes from a wide range of reputable international sources to ensure accuracy and consistency.

Dimensions and Subdimensions

Transition Readiness

Transition Readiness measures a country's ability to shift to sustainable energy by evaluating political commitment, investment access, governance, infrastructure, workforce capabilities, and the electricity mix.

Regulation and Political Commitment

Assesses policies, regulatory frameworks, electrification planning, carbon pricing, and a country's commitment to sustainable energy goals.

Adapted from the World Bank's Regulatory Indicators for Sustainable Energy (RISE)

Capital and Investment

Evaluates foreign investment, economic freedom, climate finance access, and contributions from financial institutions supporting clean energy.

Based on data from IMF, World Bank, and Heritage Foundation

Institutions and Governance

Examines government stability, regulatory effectiveness, corruption perception, and sovereign credit ratings.

Incorporates sovereign credit ratings, Rule of Law scores, and Corruption Perceptions Index

Infrastructure and Business Environment

Assesses infrastructure development, logistics performance, and the business environment for clean energy innovation.

Based on AfDB, World Bank, and WIPO data

Human Capital

Evaluates STEM education, workforce skills, research investment, and public spending on education.

Data from ILO, UNESCO, OECD, and World Bank

Power System Structure

Analyzes the electricity generation mix, per capita generation, and the balance between supply and demand.

Data from IEA, IRENA, and EMBER

System Performance

System Performance Index measures the reliability, sustainability, accessibility, and security of a country's energy system.

Economic Development and Growth

Assesses power reliability, affordability, net imports, and financial impacts of energy system inefficiencies.

Based on SAIDI, SAIFI, and World Bank data

Environmental Sustainability

Evaluates energy intensity, carbon emissions, air pollution, and biodiversity impacts.

Data from World Bank and IEA

Energy Access and Security

Examines electricity access, energy diversity, grid resilience, mini-grid development, and energy import dependency.

Based on World Bank, RISE, IEA, and AMDA data

Technology-Specific Preparedness

Technology-Specific Preparedness evaluates a country's readiness to implement clean energy technologies based on regulatory support, investment, and infrastructure.

Solar Technologies

Assesses installed capacity, investment, land and water use, and regulatory support for photovoltaic and concentrated solar power based on World Bank Global Solar Atlas data.

Key indicators include average theoretical, practical, and economic potential, installed PV capacity, and investment in solar PV energy.

Wind Turbines

Evaluates installed capacity, investment, regulatory support, and wind resource potential using data from IEA, IRENA, and Global Wind Atlas.

Measures indicators like installed wind capacity, share of total capacity, investment, LCOE, and public acceptance.

Hydropower

Examines installed capacity, investment, water stress, regulatory support, and flood risk based on World Resources Institute Aqueduct data.

Considers factors like baseline water stress, drought risk, riverine flooding, and public acceptance.

Natural Gas Turbine Power

Examines installed capacity, investment, reserves, land use, and liquefied natural gas trade using BP Statistical Review and IEA data.

Geothermal Power

Assesses installed capacity, investment, land and water use, and regulatory support based on IRENA and International Geothermal Association data.

Nuclear Power

Evaluates installed capacity, investment, regulatory frameworks, uranium resources, and public acceptance using IAEA and OECD data.

Biomass-Based Power

Assesses installed capacity, investment, land use, and regulatory support for bioenergy from various sources.

Hydrogen

Evaluates production capacity, investment, regulatory frameworks, and infrastructure for hydrogen development based on IEA data.

Carbon Capture and Storage

Examines investment, installed capacity, regulatory support, and CO₂ reduction targets using Global CCS Institute data.

Scoring Framework

Each dimension and subdimension is evaluated on a scale of 0-100, with higher scores indicating better performance:

  • System Performance (0-100): Measures the current efficiency, reliability, and coverage of energy systems
  • Transition Readiness (0-100): Assesses preparation for transitioning to sustainable energy sources
  • Tech Preparedness (0-100): Evaluates technological capacity for implementing modern energy solutions

Subdimensions are weighted based on their relative importance to create the aggregate scores. The final country score is a composite of these three dimensions, providing a comprehensive assessment of each nation's energy transition preparedness.
